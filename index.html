Prophet Muhammad ﷺ Quotes

Prophet Muhammad Life Time

1

Early Life

Born in Mecca around 570 CE, Muhammad belonged to the Quraysh tribe. He was orphaned at a young age and raised by his grandfather and later his uncle.

2

Character and Reputation:

Known as "Al-Amin" (the trustworthy), he was respected for his honesty and integrity. Before prophethood, he worked as a merchant and was known for his fair dealings.

3

Revelation

At the age of 40, Muhammad received his first revelation from the Angel Gabriel in the Cave of Hira. This marked the beginning of his role as the final prophet in Islam.

4

Message of Islam:

He preached monotheism, social justice, and moral integrity. His teachings emphasized compassion, equality, and the importance of community.

5

Persecution

Early Muslims faced severe persecution from the Quraysh, leading to the migration (Hijrah) to Medina in 622 CE, which marks the beginning of the Islamic calendar.

6

Medina Period

In Medina, Muhammad established a Muslim community and served as a political leader. He drafted the Constitution of Medina, which outlined the rights and responsibilities of all citizens.

7

Battles

Key battles such as Badr, Uhud, and the Battle of the Trench were significant in establishing the Muslim community's strength and resilience.

8

Conquest of Mecca

In 630 CE, Muhammad and his followers peacefully conquered Mecca, leading to the widespread acceptance of Islam.

9

Final Sermon

During his last pilgrimage in 632 CE, he delivered a powerful sermon emphasizing equality, justice, and the importance of following the teachings of Islam.

10

Legacy

Muhammad's teachings and practices (Sunnah) continue to guide millions of Muslims worldwide. His life is seen as a model for ethical conduct and spiritual guidance.
